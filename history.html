Our History

Over four decades of academic excellence, Christian values, and transformative education from a humble beginning in 1979 to one of Kenya's most prestigious schools.

Moi High School Kabarak is a co-educational school located in Kabarak, Nakuru County, Kenya, with a rich and inspiring history. Founded in 1979 as a mixed boarding school by the late President Daniel arap Moi one of Kenya's longest-serving leaders the school was named after the nearby Kabarak Farm owned by President Moi. What began as a small institution with a vision to serve Kenya's youth has grown into one of the country's most celebrated schools.

A Journey Through Time

1979

The Founding of MHSK

Moi High School Kabarak was established as a mixed boarding school by the Late President Daniel arap Moi, one of Kenya's longest-serving leaders. Named after the nearby Kabarak Farm, the school opened its doors with a small student population and a small but dedicated teaching staff and a powerful vision: to provide affordable, quality education to Kenyan children and prepare them for college and life.

Foundation
1980s

Growing Reputation

In its early years, the school built a steady reputation for academic discipline and Christian values. Student numbers grew as word spread across Kenya of the school's commitment to holistic education. The school's close proximity to Kabarak Farm gave it a unique identity tied to national leadership and vision.

Growth
1990s

Expansion: Primary School & Kindergarten

A landmark decade of expansion: MHSK introduced a primary school section, followed later by a kindergarten. The primary school has since become a key feeder institution into the high school. A new, forward-looking curriculum was also introduced, emphasising holistic education aimed at producing well-rounded students with strong leadership skills.

Expansion
2002

A New Era: Board of Governors

A pivotal turning point in the school's history. Following President Moi's retirement from politics, management of the school was handed over to a Board of Governors. Simultaneously, the school began registering students directly with the Ministry of Education marking the end of an era as a purely private institution and the beginning of a new chapter of wider public accountability.

Transition
2000s2010s

International Academic Excellence

MHSK established itself on the world stage, sending students to represent Kenya in prestigious international competitions including the World Scholar's Cup, the International Mathematical Olympiad, and the International Physics Olympiad. Academic and sporting achievements at both national and international levels cemented the school's status as one of Kenya's finest.

International Recognition
Ongoing

A Legacy of Notable Alumni

Over the decades, MHSK has produced a large number of successful graduates who have gone on to excel in medicine, politics, business, education, and many other fields. The school's culture of fostering leadership, innovation, and entrepreneurship has created an alumni network that is highly respected across Kenya and beyond providing mentorship and networking opportunities for current students.

Alumni Legacy

MHSK Today

Moi High School Kabarak today stands as a thriving institution with over 2,000 students, both male and female, offering a wide range of academic programs and extracurricular activities including sports, music, and drama. Despite significant changes over the decades, the school's core commitment to provide affordable, holistic, Christian-based education that produces well-rounded individuals has never wavered.
